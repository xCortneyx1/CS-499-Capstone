# Professional Self-Assessment

### Cortney Messinger  
Bachelor of Science in Computer Science – Software Engineering  
Southern New Hampshire University  
Date: February 16, 2026  

---

Completing the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University has transformed my understanding of software development from simply writing functional code to designing comprehensive, secure, and maintainable computing systems. Over the course of the program, I developed a deep appreciation for the integration of software engineering principles, algorithmic design, database systems, and security-focused thinking. Each course built upon the previous one, reinforcing the importance of structured problem-solving, thoughtful system architecture, and professional communication.

One of the most significant areas of growth throughout the program has been my ability to collaborate and communicate within technical environments. Computer science is often perceived as an isolated discipline, but in practice, it requires clear communication with peers, stakeholders, and team members. Through peer discussions, instructor feedback, and structured code reviews, I refined my ability to articulate complex technical concepts in a clear and professional manner. The informal code review created for this capstone project strengthened this skill further. Explaining system architecture, identifying design flaws, and outlining enhancement strategies required clarity, organization, and audience awareness. These experiences prepared me to function effectively in collaborative development settings where communication directly impacts organizational decision making.

From a software engineering perspective, I gained substantial experience in modular design, separation of concerns, lifecycle management, and maintainability. The Smart Thermostat artifact used in this capstone initially functioned correctly but lacked the structural clarity expected of production-level software. Through refactoring and architectural refinement, I implemented clearer boundaries between system components, centralized configuration handling, and improved error management. These changes enhanced readability, scalability, and long-term maintainability. More importantly, they reinforced the principle that well-designed software is not only functional but also organized, extensible, and resilient.

My development in algorithms and data structures is reflected in the refinement of the thermostat’s finite state machine and decision logic. Rather than relying on loosely connected conditional statements, I strengthened state transitions, validated user input boundaries, and improved logical predictability. These modifications required evaluating design trade-offs between simplicity and robustness. I learned that algorithmic correctness must be paired with efficiency and clarity. The enhancement process demonstrated my ability to evaluate computing solutions, identify inefficiencies, and implement structured improvements grounded in computer science principles.

Database integration further expanded the complexity and realism of the project. By introducing persistent storage and timestamped data logging, I transitioned the system from a transient in-memory model to a more complete and practical application. Implementing structured data persistence required consideration of schema organization, validation before insertion, and controlled access patterns. This experience strengthened my understanding of how software systems interact with data in real-world environments. It also emphasized the importance of maintaining data integrity and traceability within applications that rely on historical records.

A critical component of my growth throughout the program has been the development of a security mindset. Secure coding is not simply about encryption or authentication; it is about anticipating failure, validating assumptions, and designing defensively. In my capstone enhancements, I incorporated input validation, boundary enforcement, controlled configuration management, and structured error handling to reduce the likelihood of runtime failure or data corruption. In the database enhancement specifically, I ensured that data was validated before being written to persistent storage and that access patterns minimized unintended modification. These decisions reflect an awareness of adversarial thinking and demonstrate my commitment to designing reliable and secure systems.

Beyond technical skills, this program shaped my professional values. I learned that quality software development requires patience, iteration, and continuous refinement. Early in the program, my focus was primarily on achieving functionality. As I progressed, I began prioritizing readability, maintainability, efficiency, and security. This shift represents a maturation in my approach to computing. I now view every design decision as part of a broader system where trade-offs must be evaluated thoughtfully.

The artifacts included in this ePortfolio collectively demonstrate this progression. By enhancing a single foundational system across three domains, including software engineering, algorithms and data structures, and databases, I intentionally showcased layered growth rather than isolated improvements. Each enhancement builds upon the previous one, reflecting how real-world systems evolve over time. This integrated approach highlights my ability to analyze an existing system, identify opportunities for improvement, and implement meaningful, structured enhancements aligned with industry standards.

Completing this capstone has reinforced my readiness to enter the software development field. I have demonstrated the ability to design and evaluate computing solutions, communicate technical decisions clearly, implement structured and secure systems, and integrate multiple computer science disciplines within a cohesive project. The ePortfolio not only represents the culmination of my academic journey but also serves as tangible evidence of my technical competence, adaptability, and commitment to professional growth.

These enhancements collectively demonstrate my achievement of the Computer Science program outcomes, including collaborative communication, professional documentation, algorithmic problem solving, application of industry-standard practices, and the development of a security-focused mindset.

As I move forward in my career, I will carry forward the principles emphasized throughout this program: structured design, thoughtful problem-solving, secure development practices, and clear communication. This portfolio reflects not only what I have learned but also how I apply that knowledge with discipline, technical depth, and professional accountability.
